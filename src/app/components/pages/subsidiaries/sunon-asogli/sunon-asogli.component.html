Efficient Thermal Ernergy Production

Top executives of Shenzhen Energy first visited Ghana in late 2006 at the invitation of Togbe Afede XIV to investigate the investment climate and the power market in Ghana.

In December 2007, Shenzhen Energy and CADFUND finally decided to invest in Ghana. Thus, the first independent power producer (IPP), Sunon Asogli Power Ghana Ltd, was established to build, own and operate a 560MW power plant in Kpone, near Tema, comprising: Phase I: 2X100 MW gas-steam combined cycle generation power units Phase II: 2X360 MW gas-steam combined cycle generation power units

The two phases have been put into full commercial operation, and more than US$800 million has been invested. Plans are underway to build the first wind farm (50MW) in the country, as well as a 2,000MW coal-fired power plant, that will not only bring down the cost of power, but make Ghana a net exporter of power in the sub-region.
